Reference: Sharma, S., et al. (2024).  |  https://www.mdpi.com/3282390
Abstract:

This paper reviews how artificial intelligence is transforming both pharmacogenomics and gene-editing technologies such as CRISPR. The authors describe how AI models predict how individuals may respond to medications based on their genetic profiles, enabling more personalized and safer drug therapies. Additionally, the article explains how machine learning assists in designing and optimizing CRISPR guide sequences, improving accuracy and reducing off-target effects. The review highlights the benefits of integrating AI with experimental approaches, noting that computational tools can guide laboratory decisions and reduce development time. It also addresses challenges such as algorithmic bias, ethical considerations, and the need for standardized datasets. The authors conclude that AI will play a central role in advancing precision medicine through both drug response prediction and more efficient gene editing.
